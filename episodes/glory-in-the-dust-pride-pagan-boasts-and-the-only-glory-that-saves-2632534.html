Episode Description

In this episode of Glory in the Dust, Charles Wiese confronts the most celebrated and most lethal sin: pride. From the garden’s ancient whisper to modern banners of white pride and American pride, we expose the lie that righteousness can come from flesh, nation, achievement, or identity.


With Scripture as our plumb line and Luther as our hammer, we hear the Reformer’s warning that boasting in bloodline or ethnicity is really boasting in inherited sin from Adam, and that every boast not rooted in Christ is rebellion dressed in glory.


Pagan cultures praise self-exaltation and despise humility, but Christ descends to save the lowly, killing pride at the cross and giving the sinner a new name, not earned but bled for.


The only boast that saves is the one that ruins the boaster and glorifies the Savior:


Christ crucified for sinners.


Come for truth that cuts. Stay for mercy that saves.

“Let the one who boasts, boast in the Lord.” (1 Corinthians 1:31)
