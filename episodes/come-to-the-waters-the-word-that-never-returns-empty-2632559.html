Episode Description

A sermon on Isaiah 55:1–13, proclaimed in the Sword-of-God cadence. Christ stands at the center as the free feast of God for thirsty sinners. This message unfolds the everlasting covenant in David’s greater Son, the futility of self-purchased salvation, and the divine power of the Word that never returns empty but performs the Lord’s saving will. Preached for the pulpit, shaped by the Scriptures and the Lutheran Confessions, this sermon calls the poor, the wounded, and the grieving to the waters of Baptism, Absolution, and the Gospel’s unbreakable promise. The Word accomplishes its mission—faith comes by hearing—creation itself will one day sing at its triumph. Come, drink freely, and live.
