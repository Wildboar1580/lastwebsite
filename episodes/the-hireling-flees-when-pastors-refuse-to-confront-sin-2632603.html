Episode Description

In this episode, we confront one of the most dangerous crises in the Church today: pastors who behave like hirelings rather than shepherds. Christ warns that the hireling sees the wolf coming and flees—leaving the sheep to be devoured. This sermon exposes the deadly reality that any sin excused, minimized, or explained away becomes a mortal sin, not because God is unwilling to forgive, but because defended sin rejects His forgiveness.


We explore why faithful pastors must rebuke, discipline, and speak with the authority of God’s Word—even when the sins in question are popular, socially accepted, or deeply rooted in congregational life. Silence does not save. Silence makes shepherds partakers in the sins of their flock.


This episode is a call to courage for pastors, a call to repentance for congregations, and a reminder that Christ the Good Shepherd lays down His life for sinners—not to leave them in their sin, but to deliver them from it.
