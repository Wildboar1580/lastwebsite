Episode Description

In this episode, we explore one of the strangest and most awe-inspiring moments in the Gospels—Matthew 27:52–53, where the graves open and many saints rise from the dead at the moment of Christ’s crucifixion. What actually happened? Why does Matthew include it? And how did Martin Luther understand this extraordinary event?

Drawing from Luther’s Postils, Easter sermons, and his teaching on Christ’s victory over death, we unpack this passage as the cosmic proclamation that the kingdom of death has fallen. These risen saints are not myth, metaphor, or mystery—they are the first visible shockwave of the new creation, the ringing bells of Easter Sunday, the first trophies of Christ’s triumph. A theologically rich, pastoral, and Christ-centered journey into one of Scripture’s most neglected texts.
