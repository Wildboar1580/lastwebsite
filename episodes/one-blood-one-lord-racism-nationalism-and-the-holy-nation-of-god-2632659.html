Episode Description

This episode confronts the sins of racism, nationalism, and hatred of the outsider through the Scriptures, the Book of Concord, and the writings of Martin Luther and C.F.W. Walther.


We explore how the Bible teaches both the dignity of all peoples—“made from one blood”—and the reality that the Christian Church is a holy nation, not defined by ethnicity or earthly borders but by Baptism, faith, and the Gospel.


In this expanded episode we examine:

• God’s creation of all nations from one blood (Acts 17).

• Israel’s election as the line of Christ—not racial superiority (Deut. 7).

• The Church as the holy nation of 1 Peter 2:9.

• Luther’s commentary on the Tower of Babel and the sin of national pride.

• Luther’s warnings against despising foreigners and immigrants, since Christians are “pilgrims and strangers” on earth.

• God’s commands to love the sojourner and His judgments on nations that mistreat immigrants (Lev. 19, Ex. 22, Mal. 3).

• Walther’s rejection of racial pride and nationalism as idolatry.

• Why racism, nationalism, and hatred of immigrants are violations of the First, Fifth, and Eighth Commandments.


This is not politics. This is the Law and the Gospel, a call to repentance, and a confession of the one holy nation created by the blood of Christ.
