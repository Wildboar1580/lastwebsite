Episode Description

When Tolerated Sin Becomes Mortal Sin is a bold, confessional episode that confronts one of the most dangerous lies in modern Christianity: the idea that sins can be managed, excused, or tolerated without destroying faith. Drawing deeply from Holy Scripture and the Lutheran Confessions, this episode explains why any sin that is defended or left unrepented becomes a mortal sin—not because it is unusually shocking, but because it extinguishes faith by rejecting repentance.


This episode also addresses the grave responsibility placed upon pastors. According to God’s Word and the Confessions, shepherds who refuse to warn their people—even about common or socially acceptable sins—become sharers in those sins through their silence. Covering passages from Ezekiel, the Epistles, and the Augsburg Confession, this podcast exposes false mercy, sentimental love, and pastoral cowardice, while calling the Church back to true repentance and true Gospel comfort.


This is not an episode about shame—but about salvation. Where sin is named, Christ is proclaimed. Where repentance is preached, forgiveness is real. Where the Law does its killing work, the Gospel raises the dead.
