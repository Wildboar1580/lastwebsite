Episode Description

What were the real sins of Sodom? Scripture gives a fuller picture than the modern debates allow. In this episode, we explore the biblical testimony—from Genesis, Ezekiel, Isaiah, Jude, and the teachings of the Church—showing that Sodom’s wickedness was more than sexual perversion. At its core was the same root that corrupts every nation and every heart: the love of self, the hatred of the other, and the rejection of God.


We trace how America mirrors these sins today—not only in exporting pornography and LGBT ideology, but also in our national pride, injustice, and hostility toward the outsider. We examine the government’s biblical responsibilities toward sojourners, how American foreign policy creates the very outsiders we later despise, and why Christians should see themselves as pilgrims rather than cultural gatekeepers.


This episode calls the Church to repentance, compassion, and courage, reminding us that Christ rescues Sodom-like sinners and forms His people into a community of truth and love.
