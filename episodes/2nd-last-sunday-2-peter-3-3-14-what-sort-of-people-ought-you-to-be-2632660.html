Episode Description

In this episode, we explore St. Peter’s powerful warning and comfort in 2 Peter 3:3–14 as the Church approaches the end of the Church Year. Scripture teaches that scoffers will arise, the world will mock Christ’s return, and everything we cling to will one day be dissolved in the fire of God’s final judgment. But for the Christian, this is not a message of fear—it is a message of hope, repentance, and holy expectation.


Why does the Lord delay His coming? What does it mean that we are “not destined for wrath”? And what kind of lives should Christians live as we await the new heavens and the new earth where righteousness dwells?


This sermon calls us to sober watchfulness, confident faith, and lives of holiness grounded in Christ’s mercy.


Scripture: 2 Peter 3:3–14

Theme: “What Sort of People Ought You to Be?”
