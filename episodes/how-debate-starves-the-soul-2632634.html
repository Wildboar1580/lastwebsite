Episode Description

In a world addicted to arguing—especially on social media—Jesus gives us a command that many Christians regularly break: “Do not give dogs what is holy; do not throw your pearls before pigs.” (Matthew 7:6)


This episode explores why continuing to debate fools, mockers, and hardened hearts is not a sign of faithfulness, but a temptation toward self-justification and spiritual immaturity. We’ll look at:


Why Jesus sometimes refused to speak


How arguing corrodes humility and faith


Why “winning” an online debate may actually be a sin


How silence can be a confession of trust in Christ


The difference between evangelism and ego


Why spiritually mature Christians know when to stop talking



The Gospel is treasure. It is not entertainment. And Christ does not ask you to win arguments—He asks you to trust the Word and obey Him even when that means walking away in silence.


Sometimes the holiest thing you can say… is nothing.
