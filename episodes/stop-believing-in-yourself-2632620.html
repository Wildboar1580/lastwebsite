Episode Description

Modern America tells us to “follow your dreams,” “trust your heart,” and “believe in yourself.” But Scripture teaches the exact opposite: do not trust in yourself at all. True humility is not personality style or emotional softness—it is absolute dependence on Christ alone.


In this episode, we examine the Bible’s theology of humility and its conflict with both pagan virtue and American values. We explore how the cross destroys our self-confidence, how suffering teaches humility, and how only Christ gives us His humility through Word and Sacrament.


Featuring direct quotations from Martin Luther, this episode confronts the myths of the “self-made man,” exposes the idolatry of personal greatness, and proclaims the good news: you don’t have to believe in yourself—Christ is enough.


> “We are beggars. This is true.” —Martin Luther
