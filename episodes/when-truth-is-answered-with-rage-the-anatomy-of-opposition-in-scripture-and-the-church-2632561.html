Episode Description

Why is truth so often met with shouting instead of reasoning, accusations instead of arguments, and violence instead of debate?


In this episode, Charles Wiese walks through Scripture and church history to expose a consistent pattern: those who oppose God’s truth frequently abandon reason and respond with insults, false charges, emotional outbursts, and force. From Pharaoh and the prophets, to Christ and the apostles, to the martyrs and Reformers, the same reaction appears again and again.

This episode also uncovers the deeper issue beneath the hostility—fear and insecurity. Those who appear most aggressive are often the most uncertain, and their rage reveals not confidence but terror that their beliefs cannot withstand examination.


A theologically rich, Scripture-saturated episode for Christians who are tired of being told that calm conviction is arrogance and who want to understand why truth provokes such hostility—and how to respond with faithfulness, clarity, and courage.
