Episode Description

Many claim Romans 13 doesn’t apply to Americans because we elect our leaders and don’t have a king. But this episode shows from Scripture and history that authority comes from God alone—not from voters, not from “consent of the governed,” and not from Enlightenment philosophy.


We’ll explore:

• How Rome had elections and mixed government when Paul wrote Romans 13

• Why “government by consent” contradicts the Bible

• How people don’t truly behave as though they consent

• How God identifies rulers by the authority He gives, not by human approval

• How God uses even tyrants to maintain order

• And how God’s warning in 1 Samuel 8 about kings perfectly describes American presidents


A theologically rich, Scripture-driven exploration of authority, vocation, and God’s ordering of civil life.
